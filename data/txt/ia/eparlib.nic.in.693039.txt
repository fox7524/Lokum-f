CSR and Social Audit 

Ramachandran Shri Krishnan Narayanasamy 


Will the Minister of CORPORATE AFFAIRS be pleased to state: 


(a) whether the Government have insisted the corporate companies of the country on implementation of Corporate Social 
Responsibility (CSR); 


(b) if so, the details thereof and the response of the corporate companies thereon; and 


(c) whether the Government plans to undertake a social audit of good governance practices in the corporate sector and if so, the 
details thereof? 


Answer 


THE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE 
MINISTRY OF CORPORATE AFFAIRS (SHRI ARJUN RAM MEGHWAL) 


(a) & (0): Section 35 of the Companies Act 203, requires every company above the specified thresholds of turnover, or net worth, or 
net profit, to spend at least two per cent of the average net profits earned during three immediately preceding financial years, on 
Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR). Section 35 of the Companies Act 2073, the Rules made thereunder, and Schedule VII of the 
Act read with General Circulars dated 78th June, 204 and 72th January, 206 issued by the Ministry of Corporate Affairs, give the 
broad contour within which the Board of the eligible companies have been empowered to formulate their CSR policies and take 
decision with respect to allocation of CSR funds across various development sectors.
