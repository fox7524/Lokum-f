[17th May, 2000] RAJYA SABHA 


Purchase of wireless sets by Delhi Police 

5548. SHRI AMAR SINGH: Will the Minister of HOME AFFAIRS be 
pleased to state: 

(a) whether Delhi Police purchased 286 wireless handsets and its 
accessories in May, 1999; 

(b) if so, the expenditure incurred thereon; 

(c) whether it is a fact that most of the wireless so purchased are highly 
defective; < 

(d) if so, the details thereof; 

(e) whether Government have fixed up the responsibility against the 
officials who purchased the items; 

(f) if so, the details thereof; and 

(g) if not, the reasons therefor? 

THE MINISTER OF STATE.IN THE MINISTRY OF HOME AFFAIRS 
(SHRI CH. VIDYASAGAR RAO): (a) to (g) Delhi Police last year purchaséd 286 
hand held wirless sets alongwith batteries at the total cost of Rs. 49,43,510/-. The 
delivery of these sets was received partly in April, 1999 and partly in July, 
1999, Delhi Police have instituted a vigilance inquiry into the said purchase. 

Formation of Autonomous Council 

5549. SHRI DAWA LAMA: Will the Minister of HOME AFFAIRS be 
pleased to state: 

(a) whether there is any scope to form an autonomous council under 
Sixth Schedule of Indian Constitution without amending it; 

(b) whether "Police" can be put under any autonomous council by any 
State Government without amendment of State-list of the Constitution; and 

(c) if replies of (a) and (b) above aré the affirmative, what are provisions 
to protect citizens from misuse of powers? . 

THE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HOMEAFFAIRS 
(SHRI I.D. SWAMI): (a) No, Sir. 

(b) Yes, Sir 

(c) Citizens of India are protected from misuse of powers through the 


Fundamental Rights guaranteed under the Constitution of India and an 
independent judiciary.
